Energy

Energy is created by power sources such as steam engines, solar power and burning oil.

Charcoal Engine: 0

Burns charcoal to produce a steady source of energy.
Each engine produces 1 energy per second.
Costs 50 Metal, 25 Gems.

Solar Panels: 0

Gains energy slowly from the sun without using any resources.
Each Solar Panel produces 0.5 energy per second.
Costs 30 Metal, 35 Gems.

Methane Power Stations: 0

Burn powerful methane from Venus to satisfy all your power needs.
Each Power Station produces 8 energy and uses 6 methane per second.
Costs 50 Space Metal, 40 Titanium.

Oil

Oil is pumped up from the ground and is used to build most machines that gather resources and also as a way of producing power.

Storage Upgrade

Upgrade your oil storage size to 100.
Costs 50 oil, 20 metal.

Small Pump: 0

Build a small pump to extract oil from the ground
Costs 60 Metal, 20 Gems.

Pumpjack: 0

Pumpjacks are much bigger than small pumps and produce oil on an industrial scale but they require a lot of energy. Each one produces 5 oil for 4 energy every second.
Costs 250 metal, 80 gems, 50 oil.

Rocket Fuel

Rocket fuel is created in chemical plants and is used to allow rockets to launch off into space.

Chemical Plant: 0

Chemical plants are used to make rocket fuel automatically. They require 100 oil and 100 charcoal to produce 1 rocket fuel. However, this is done slowly in 0.2 increments as the proccess takes 5 seconds for each plant to complete.
Costs 1,000 Metal, 750 Gems, 500 Oil.



Rocket

Building a rocket will allow for space exploration and will allow you to gather resources in space.

Rocket: 0

To launch the rocket into space, it must first be built.
Costs 1,200 Metal, 900 Gems, 1,000 Oil.



Launch Rocket

Launching the rocket into space will unlock space mining, exploration and other planets.
This requires 1 rocket and 20 rocket fuel.



The Moon

The Moon is our largest satelite, revolving around the Earth once every 27 days. It contains a large quantity of space metal, left over from when it separated from the Earth billions of years ago. It is much stronger than regular metal and used to build more advanced machines.

Exploration

Exploring the Moon will unlock the collection of space metal.
Costs 20 rocket fuel.



Venus

Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system and the closest one to Earth. It is rich in greenhouse gases, contributing to its extreme heat. You can extract methane from Venus, which allows you to build up a new form of power generation.

Exploration

Exploring Venus will unlock methane and methane based power.
Costs 50 rocket fuel.



Mars

Mars is the second closest planet to Earth and is covered in red iron oxide, also called rust. You can find titanium on the surface, but it is more common inside the crust.

Exploration

Exploring Mars unlocks titanium production, which allows you to harvest silicon on Earth and to build methane power plants.
Costs 80 rocket fuel.



Asteroid Belt

The asteroid belt is a vast space with nearly 2 million asteroids. There you can harvest rare metals on Earth more easily, such as gold and silver.

Exploration

Exploring the asteroid belt gives you access to gold and silver.
Costs 200 rocket fuel.

Beginner's Guide

This guide will get you from starting the game to your first prestige and will be helpful to grasp everything you need to do. You can refer to this guide at anytime while playing by clicking the Help tab in the top right and selecting the Getting Started sub-tab.

First Steps

The first thing you can do is start gathering resources. To do this, click the Resources tab near the top left of the screen. This will bring you to the resources display. You can click any of the resources to access its interface, where you can mine the resource yourself by clicking, or you can hire people or build machines to gain those resources for you. While you can hire workers for all three resources, it is recommended to prioritise Metal and Wood in the early stages of the game.

Research

To unlock research, you must purchase a metal miner, costing 10 metal and 5 wood. This is the first step to idle automation. The Research tab is where you can build laboratories to produce science for you, and also where you can discover and research new technologies to further help you through the game.

The first technology you can research is 'Storage Upgrades', which allows you to build storages to hold more of each type of resource. This is necessary when building machines and other tools costs more than 50 of a resource. You can also research 'Basic Energy Production', which opens up the world of power and advanced machines to help you get resources even faster.

Balancing Resources

This game has different amounts output by each machine. This can make balancing your resources hard, especially your energy, charcoal and wood as they are consumed by other machines. As at tip, prioritise which resources you should build machines for first - in most cases metal - and produce the 2 energy required for a heavy drill. Don't upgrade everything to machines at once as you will end up with an energy deficit (this is bad as the machines won't work without energy). Build up your charcoal to build engines or increase the number of solar panels to counter this.

Space

The first thing needed to unlock space exploration is to build a rocket. This is the first large goal that you should aim for as a target. To build a rocket, you need a lot of resources, which you most likely need to use advanced machines to get without taking an extremely long time. Secondly, to launch it requires 20 rocket fuel, which can be made in chemical plants. These require a large amount of oil and charcoal to produce - 20 of each per second per plant. Once you have 20 rocket fuel and have built a rocket, press the launch button to unlock space travel and new technologies and ways to get resources.

About

This game made by me, Sparticle999, as a project to improve my skills at making games and to see what I could add to the incremental gaming world.
The content in this game is not always accurate. This is because if it were accurate, many of the elements on other planets would be the same as Earth's, such as metal on Mars (iron oxide). Thus, I have had to make up some facts to help my game stay diverse. I appologise for any infuriation caused.

Tools Used

Bootstrap was central to the design of the game, and until I decide to create an interface for myself, either when I feel that it is necessary, or when people ask me to change it, this will be the general look for a while.

Most of the images are from Shuttershock and retextured and edited by myself to fit the theme of this game.
