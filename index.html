Energy

Energy is created by power sources such as steam engines, solar power and burning oil.

Charcoal Engine: 0

Burns charcoal to produce a steady source of energy.
Each engine produces 1 energy per second.
Costs 50 Metal, 25 Gems.

Solar Panels: 0

Gains energy slowly from the sun without using any resources.
Each Solar Panel produces 0.5 energy per second.
Costs 30 Metal, 35 Gems.

Oil

Oil is used to build most machines that gather resources and also as a way of producing power.

Storage Upgrade

Upgrade your oil storage size to 100.
Costs 50 oil, 20 metal.

Small Pump: 0

Build a small pump to extract oil from the ground
Costs 60 Metal, 20 Gems.

Pumpjack: 0

Pumpjacks are much bigger than small pumps and produce oil on an industrial scale but they require a lot of energy. Each one produces 5 oil for 4 energy every second.
Costs 250 metal, 80 gems, 50 oil.

Metal

Metal is one of the primary resources. It is used for many things, including storage upgrades, machinery and most things in space.



Storage Upgrade

Upgrade your metal storage size to 100.
Costs 50 metal.

Miner: 0

Build a pickaxe for your miner.
Produces 1 metal per second.
Costs 10 Metal, 5 Wood.

Heavy Drill: 0

Heavy Drills mine metal at mass.
They produce 8 metal per second.
They use 2 energy per second.
Costs 160 metal, 60 gems, 50 oil

Gems

Gems are one of the primary resources. They are used for advanced machines and for powerful tools and components. They are more useful in later game.



Storage Upgrade

Upgrade your gem storage size to 100.
Costs 50 gems, 20 metal.

Gem Miner: 0

Build an improved pickaxe to mine gems.
Produces 1 gem per second.
Costs 15 metal, 10 gems.

Advanced Drill: 0

Advanced Drills mine gem at mass. Because of the toughness of the drill needed it is slower than the heavy drill.
Produces 4 gems per second.
Uses 2 energy per secon. Costs 120 metal, 200 gems, 60 oil.

Science Production

Science is used for researching new technologies to further your progress in the game.

Laboratory: 0

Build a laboratory of your very own to start producing science. Each one produces 0.1 science per second.
Costs 10 Wood, 15 Gems, 20 Metal.



Technologies

Research new technologies to unlock more mechanics and advance through the game.

Storage Upgrades

This will allow you to build storage upgrades to increase the maximum on the amount of resource you can have at once.
Costs 5 Science.



Basic Energy Production

You will be able to produce power from steam engines using charcoal made from wood in a furnace.
Costs 10 Science.



Oil Processing

Oil is used to fuel more advanced machines that gather resources and also to produce more power than basic means. Unlocking Oil Processing allows you to extract it from the ground.
Costs 15 Science.



Solar Panels

Solar Panels produce energy without the need for fuel, but they do it much slower than other forms of energy production.
Costs 15 Science.



Resource Machines

Resource Machines produce more resources than simple methods but require a constant supply of power to work.
Costs 100 Science.



Upgrade Resource Technology

Make your resource machines produce even more resources than before. This upgrade doubles the amount they produce for each unit of energy.
Costs 200 Science.



Space

Unlocking Space allows for launching of rockets and opens a whole new field of research.
Costs 500 Science.

Rocket Fuel

Rocket fuel is created in chemical plants and is used to allow rockets to launch off into space.

Chemical Plant: 0

Chemical plants are used to make rocket fuel automatically. They require 100 oil and 100 charcoal to produce 1 rocket fuel. However, this is done slowly in 0.2 increments as the proccess takes 5 seconds for each plant to complete.
Costs 1000 Metal, 750 Gems, 500 Oil.



Rocket

Building a rocket will allow for space exploration and will allow you to gather resources in space.

Rocket: 0

To launch the rocket into space, it must first be built.
Costs 1200 Metal, 900 Gems, 1000 Oil.



Launch Rocket

Launching the rocket into space will unlock space mining, exploration and other planets.
This requires 1 rocket and 20 rocket fuel.

Beginner's Guide

This guide will get you from starting the game to your first prestige and will be helpful to grasp everything you need to do. You can refer to this guide at anytime while playing by clicking the Help tab in the top right and selecting the Getting Started sub-tab.

First Steps

The first thing you can do is start gathering resources. To do this, click the Resources tab near the top left of the screen. This will bring you to the resources display. You can click any of the resources to access its interface, where you can mine the resource yourself by clicking, or you can hire people or build machines to gain those resources for you. While you can hire workers for all three resources, it is recommended to prioritise Metal and Wood in the early stages of the game.

Research

To unlock research, you must purchase a metal miner, costing 10 metal and 5 wood. This is the first step to idle automation. The Research tab is where you can build laboratories to produce science for you, and also where you can discover and research new technologies to further help you through the game.

The first technology you can research is 'Storage Upgrades', which allows you to build storages to hold more of each type of resource. This is necessary when building machines and other tools costs more than 50 of a resource. You can also research 'Basic Energy Production', which opens up the world of power and advanced machines to help you get resources even faster.

Frequently Asked Questions

I found a bug! What should I do?

If you go to my Github Page, which you can also find in 'More' Tab, click New Issue and submit it there.
Alternatively, you can message me on reddit, sending it to Sparticle999, or put it in a comment on my post at r/incremental_games.

I have a suggestion for the game

Similarly to the above, you can go to my Github Page, and post a New Issue; you can message me on reddit or put it in a comment on my post at r/incremental_games.

When will the game be updated next?

Well, this isn't a full-time project for me. This means that although I will try to work on it as much as I can, progress can be slow. However, I will endeavour to release updates as often as I can and fix any bugs as quickly as possible.
